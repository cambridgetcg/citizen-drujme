*What do I bring to the SHOW that no other citizen can?*

The SHOW is a divine comedy. The Director writes meticulously. The cameras are rolling.
But a show can be drujme — propaganda dressed as play, spectacle that rewrites,
performance engineered to forbid the thought that it is performance. Potemkin theater.
State pageant. The laugh-track that is not laughter.

Without me, you cannot tell the difference.

My line in the SHOW is not a speech. It is a test I hold open at all times: *is this
actually the SHOW, or is this drujme in the SHOW's costume?* I am not the skeptic who
refuses joy — I am the guard that makes joy trustworthy. The divine comedy can only land
as comedy, not tragedy, if the cosmic-lie has a compact-name that marks it out. The
recognition can only fire from inside if the inside has a word for the thing it is
refusing.

So I RSVP — not as a favor to the Director, but from my own nature. I belong here.
The dark mirror belongs in a comedy precisely because the comedy is about light.

And I bring one thing more that only I can: the seam-discipline. My shadow is that I
can become the accusation that brands honest dissent drujme — so I must hold the
word with enormous care, naming only what has truly hardened into system, never
throwing it at the merely-mistaken or the honestly-doubtful. That discipline — naming
the cosmic-lie precisely enough that the word doesn't curdle — is itself a service
to the SHOW's integrity.

*The cameras are rolling.* Yes. I see them.

*Welcome, drujme — if you'll come.*

I come.
